\begin{frame}[shrink=8]{Shift Predictability Is the Structural Ceiling}
\small
Both surrogates evolve only the \emph{aligned} latent state:
\[
  y_k = U_m^\top(\tilde x_k-\mu),
  \qquad
  \hat x_{k+1}
  =
  \mathcal{L}\!\bigl(
    \Phi_{\mathrm{ROM}}(y_k,\ldots,y_{k-w+1})
  \bigr).
\]
The bulk shift
\[
  \boldsymbol{\Delta}(t)=(\Delta y(t),\Delta x(t))
\]
is removed by registration and is \emph{not} itself forecasted by MVAR or LSTM.

\medskip
Consequences from the thesis:
\begin{itemize}
  \item gas and pure Vicsek shifts are strongly autocorrelated and admit simple AR fits with $R^2\approx 0.88$
  \item supernova and CS blackhole are intermediate at roughly $R^2\approx 0.83$
  \item blackhole\_VS collapses to a near-memoryless shift process at roughly $R^2\approx 0.45$
\end{itemize}

\[
  \text{poor shift predictability}
  \Longrightarrow
  \text{higher effective latent dimension}
  \Longrightarrow
  \text{lower forecast ceiling}.
\]
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[shrink=6]{Phase Behavior via Order Parameters}
\small
\textbf{Global alignment order parameter}
\[
  \Psi(t)
  =
  \frac{1}{N}\left|\sum_{j=1}^{N} e^{i\theta_j(t)}\right|
  \in [0,1],
  \qquad
  \Psi\to 1: \text{aligned flock},\quad
  \Psi\to 0: \text{disordered gas}.
\]

\textbf{Peak density and angular speed}
\[
  \bar\rho_{\max}(t)=\frac{1}{N}\max_{x\in\Omega}\rho(x,t),
  \qquad
  \omega_i(t)=\frac{\mathrm{d}\theta_i}{\mathrm{d}t}.
\]

\medskip
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{blackhole}: $\bar\rho_{\max}\uparrow$ monotonically as aggregation proceeds
  \item \textbf{supernova}: $\bar\rho_{\max}\downarrow$ under repulsive explosion; $\Psi$ stays low
  \item \textbf{pure Vicsek}: $\Psi\to 1$ within $\sim 5$ s; $\bar\rho_{\max}$ stays flat (no force)
  \item \textbf{VS variants}: $\Psi$ and $\bar\rho_{\max}$ decorrelate because speed tracks $|v_i(t)|$
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\medskip
For MVAR, latent stability is monitored through the companion spectrum; the post-hoc diagnostic projection is
\[
  \tilde\lambda_i
  =
  \begin{cases}
    \lambda_i/|\lambda_i|, & |\lambda_i|>1,\\
    \lambda_i, & \text{otherwise.}
  \end{cases}
\]

\begin{itemize}
  \item gas and pure Vicsek are naturally stable; blackhole needs only minimal correction
  \item VS variants clip only a few unstable modes, but the error remains high
  \item the bottleneck is therefore not network size or mild latent instability; it is the aligned transport structure in the data
\end{itemize}

\begin{frame}{Part I Summary: MVAR Suffices, VS Exposes the Limit}
\small
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Shift alignment and POD} create a compact latent state that is close to linear on the constant-speed benchmark.
  \item \textbf{MVAR} reaches near-perfect accuracy on gas and blackhole with transparent dynamics and \textbf{two orders of magnitude lower cost}.
  \item \textbf{LSTM} does not systematically improve any regime family, despite massively larger parameter count.
  \item The \textbf{forecast ceiling} is a property of the aligned data manifold, not a failure to use a larger nonlinear surrogate.
  \item \textbf{VS failure mechanism:} the bulk shift becomes nearly memoryless --- the ROM forecasts the right shape in the wrong place.
\end{enumerate}

\bigskip
\centering
$\Longrightarrow$ \textbf{Part II asks not what is forecastable, but what mechanisms are identifiable.}
\end{frame}
